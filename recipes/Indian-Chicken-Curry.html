Chicken Curry Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 large onion
  • 6 garlic cloves, roughly chopped
  • 50g ginger, roughly chopped
  • 4 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp fennel seeds
  • 5cm cinnamon stick
  • 1 tsp chilli flakes
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp caster sugar
  • 400g can chopped tomatoes
  • 8 chicken thighs, skinned and boneless (about 800g)
  • 250ml hot chicken stock
  • 2 tbsp chopped coriander

Method:

  1. Roughly chop 1 large onion, transfer to a small food processor, and add 3 tbsp of water â€“ process to a slack paste. Alternatively, grate the onion coarsely. Tip into a small bowl and set aside.
  2. Put 6 roughly chopped garlic cloves and 50g roughly chopped ginger into the same food processor, add 4 tbsp water, and process until smooth. Spoon into another small bowl. Alternatively, crush the garlic and finely grate the ginger.
  3. Heat 4 tbsp vegetable oil in a wok or sturdy pan over medium heat.
  4. Combine 2 tsp cumin seeds, 1 tsp fennel seeds, a 5cm cinnamon stick, and 1 tsp chilli flakes. Add to the pan and swirl for about 30 seconds until the spices release a fragrant aroma.
  5. Add the onion paste (it will splutter initially). Fry until the water evaporates and the onions turn a dark golden color (about 7-8 minutes).
  6. Add the garlic and ginger paste and cook for another 2 minutes, stirring constantly.
  7. Stir in 1 tsp garam masala, 1 tsp turmeric, and 1 tsp caster sugar, then cook for 20 seconds before adding a 400g can of chopped tomatoes.
  8. Cook on medium heat for about 10 minutes without a lid, letting the tomatoes reduce and darken.
  9. Cut 8 skinless, boneless chicken thighs into 3cm chunks. Add them to the pan once the tomatoes have thickened into a paste.
  10. Cook the chicken for 5 minutes, coating it in the masala and sealing the juices. Then pour over 250ml hot chicken stock.
  11. Simmer for 8-10 minutes without a lid until the chicken is tender and the masala lightly thickened. Add extra stock or water if needed.
  12. Sprinkle with 2 tbsp chopped coriander and serve with Indian flatbreads, basmati rice, and a side of yogurt.
